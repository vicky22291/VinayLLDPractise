# Flipkart US R&D Billion AI 2022

> Conference Highlights

Ganapathy Krishnan, Vice President and Head, US R&D Center, Flipkart

## Billion AI

When I was a child in Mumbai, I remember going to the government ration store to purchase wheat, rice, and basic essentials. I also remember wandering around at 5:00 am looking for a quart of milk because it was in such short supply. Today, India is a very different and vibrant country, and the distribution network powered by private companies has grown tremendously. A vast group of Indians can have democratized access to products and services. Artificial Intelligence is leveling the playing field by removing the barriers of language, providing the ability to search the Internet via text, voice, and image, improving efficiency and accuracy in medical diagnostics, and more.

India’s homegrown eCommerce marketplace, Flipkart, has established a center of AI excellence in Bellevue, Washington. This is the third year of our [Billion AI, our flagship tech and networking event](./flipkart-us-r-d-billion-ai-2022-conference-6cb3e262c700.md).

We named our event Billion AI because it is truly what AI can do for over a billion people with a great deal of diversity. In this year’s event, various industry leaders gave us their insights on topics that range from the use of good data, customer experience in Walmart, Visual search, Supply chain, AR/VR, Flipkart Health, and futuristic applications of AI in eCommerce.

Practical applications of Artificial Intelligence, powered by deep learning and faster computing power mean that we can develop huge AI models with billions of parameters and train them on massive amounts of unstructured data.

## AI for Product Experience

ECommerce has been slow to take off in areas where customers need to actually experience the product before buying it. For example, how can you see what a particular shade of lipstick looks like before you buy it? It’s easy to do this in the store, but hard to do it online. Ajay Ponna Venkatesha from Flipkart Labs talks about the [exciting new applications](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fb0N7QmYzAc&list=PL8pOuZ0wD6T2b79YJ2rafINE_GRcOe0Hd&index=5) in this area from trying on makeup to seeing if the sofa you are in love with fits in your living room. There is one catch for scaling, however, and that is building 3d models more easily and cost-effectively than we can today. Our AI team works closely with Flipkart labs to dramatically reduce the cost of building 3d models.

## AI for Search

The search was one of the first practical applications of AI technologies. Flipkart has a search to match the customer query across hundreds of millions of products. Visual Search is an emerging variation that is rapidly gathering adoption because sometimes people do not know how to articulate what they are looking for. Instead, they simply want to take a photo and then search using that photo. I can see Visual Search really taking off in India, especially in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities.

In Billion AI 2022, Samir Shah from Flipkart leads a [panel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YBobxbKSbaU&list=PL8pOuZ0wD6T2b79YJ2rafINE_GRcOe0Hd&index=4) with industry experts from Microsoft (Arun Sacheti), Pinterest (Deepak Agarwal), and Google (Sachin Padwal). This is a challenge because consumers are used to textual search latencies of half a second or so. Deep Learning technology has provided very good results while still respecting latency requirements. The panelists also talk about the convergence of the embedding space that combines text, images, and voice.

## AI for Customer Service

Artificial Intelligence is a great leveler, especially for developing countries where English is not the primary language. With AI, you can engage in eCommerce in a language of your choice. Large language models especially trained for eCommerce help eCommerce companies to understand their customers’ spoken and typed intents.

Machine learning techniques such as Active Learning, help achieve a level of superior customer service, which is a direct contributor to repeat business. At Flipkart, we use chatbots to help customers with a variety of problems from understanding the status of their order to processing a return. We can also predict whether a customer is likely to experience a delay and then alert them before they actually experience the delay. We can do this at scale, which ensures that our human agents can focus on the most complex customer service interactions.

In Billion AI 2022, Nina Patel from Walmart sets the context by talking about the [customer experience at Walmart](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yP6KvU_-lKE&list=PL8pOuZ0wD6T2b79YJ2rafINE_GRcOe0Hd&index=2). Then, Dhawal Majumdar and Venkata Nadavanam talk about [Walmart’s conversational assistant](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=avRDYEA3qjc&list=PL8pOuZ0wD6T2b79YJ2rafINE_GRcOe0Hd&index=3) for customer service.

## AI for Supply Chain

The eCommerce marketplace is powered by a massively interconnected network of suppliers, manufacturers, logistics companies, warehouses, and financial credit to shoppers and sellers. A failure in one area may mean that a kid does not get his birthday present on time.

AI technologies can consume millions of data points and predict demand, warehouse inventory levels, and the correct geolocation to deliver goods. Demand by itself is very complicated; imagine a multidimensional matrix where the dimensions could be the postal code and the SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) of the item. Generating the right pattern of demand also depends on advertising, sales, when it can be delivered, and a host of other factors. The demand profile affects the entire supply network. Paying sellers in the marketplace is also very important because if they are not paid on time, they lose the working capital to buy additional inventory.

In Billion AI 2022, Dr. Surya Pathak, Professor at the University of Washington leads a very [insightful discussion](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GuZd4qxlcE8&list=PL8pOuZ0wD6T2b79YJ2rafINE_GRcOe0Hd&index=8) with industry experts from American Express (Shilpa Dhopate), Flipkart (Vikas Goel), and Chewy (Aman Goyal) about the complexity of the global supply chain and its impact on eCommerce.

## AI for Flipkart Healthcare

Flipkart has recently entered the healthcare business with the vision of providing cost-effective healthcare. This has been a traditionally underserved area. In India, most people only go to the doctor if they are not well. The concept of preventive care is catching on, but most people cannot afford to get a baseline blood test done to know if they have a high sugar level or high lipids.

Lacking this knowledge, they harm their bodies and suffer tremendously in their old age from various ailments that could have been averted by practicing a good diet and exercise, and/or remedial medication. This is an area that I see growing in leaps and bounds because of the latent demand in the Indian market. Artificial Intelligence can help make health care cost-effective in a variety of ways, from flagging incorrect prescriptions, identifying the onset of diabetes from retinal scans, providing online telemedicine, and much more. The US R&D team is engaged with the Flipkart Health team. In Billion AI 2022, Prashant Jhaveri delivers his [energizing vision](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-YvwhQysT7o&list=PL8pOuZ0wD6T2b79YJ2rafINE_GRcOe0Hd&index=6) for Flipkart health.

## AI at the Enterprise level

And finally, in our closing session of Billion AI 2022, Anusua Trivedi has an [insightful interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AWeq5UGo2D0&list=PL8pOuZ0wD6T2b79YJ2rafINE_GRcOe0Hd&index=7) with Kamelia Aryafar. Kamelia focuses on scaling at the enterprise level. She also brought up the point that the AI development process differs greatly from the engineering process and sets the right expectations.

Kamellia predicts AI will be far more accessible to many enterprises. AI is maturing and will unlock values for many more companies soon. I also liked the idea of AI helping us move to a 4-day work week, so we can pursue some of our other interests.

## AI for India

India is a vast and diverse country and is growing by leaps and bounds. AI is such a key technology for us to address the needs of this nation at a cost that people can afford. The solutions that we develop, apply to the vast majority of humanity. India will be at the forefront of these solutions in the coming decades. In my last three years at Flipkart, I have been blown away by the talent and passion of the Flipkart team in addressing the needs of a diverse population in India.

Do invest a few hours of your time in listening to these talks and you are sure to walk away inspired and motivated.
